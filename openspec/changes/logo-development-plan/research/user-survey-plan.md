# User Survey Plan: Logo Preferences

## Survey Objectives

### Primary Goals
- Understand visual preferences of Nigerian parents for educational payment platforms
- Gather insights from school administrators on professional branding needs
- Test emotional responses to security, education, and payment visual elements
- Identify cultural resonance and color preferences
- Validate design direction before concept development

## Target Audiences

### Primary: Parents
**Demographics**:
- Age: 25-55 years
- Location: Urban and semi-urban Nigeria (Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt, Ibadan)
- Education: Secondary school and above
- Income: Middle to upper-middle class
- Tech Comfort: Mobile banking users

**Sample Size**: 150-200 parents
**Recruitment**: School partnerships, parent associations, social media

### Secondary: School Administrators
**Roles**:
- School principals
- Bursars/accountants
- Administrative heads
- IT coordinators

**Sample Size**: 50-75 administrators
**Recruitment**: Direct school outreach, educational associations

## Survey Methodology

### Phase 1: Online Survey
**Platform**: Google Forms / SurveyMonkey
**Duration**: 10-15 minutes
**Incentive**: ₦1,000 gift card for completed surveys

### Phase 2: Focus Groups
**Format**: Virtual and in-person sessions
**Duration**: 45-60 minutes
**Groups**: 6-8 participants per session
**Incentive**: ₦5,000 honorarium

### Phase 3: One-on-One Interviews
**Participants**: 15-20 key stakeholders
**Duration**: 30 minutes
**Focus**: Deep dive into preferences and concerns

## Survey Content

### Section A: Demographics
- Age, gender, location
- Education level, income range
- Children's education level
- Digital payment experience
- Current payment methods for school fees

### Section B: Current Perceptions
- Awareness of existing payment platforms
- Trust factors in payment systems
- Concerns about digital payments
- Security expectations
- Brand recognition factors

### Section C: Visual Preferences
**Image-Based Questions**:
- Show 10-15 existing fintech/edtech logos
- Rate on: Trust, Professionalism, Security, Approachability
- Rank preferences
- Explain reasoning

**Color Preferences**:
- Show color palette options
- Rate emotional responses (trust, security, excitement, professionalism)
- Cultural appropriateness assessment

### Section D: Symbol Testing
**Security Symbols**:
- Shield variations (traditional, modern, abstract)
- Lock symbols (padlock, keyhole, integrated)
- Enclosure shapes (circles, squares, abstract)

**Education Symbols**:
- Graduation caps, books, scrolls
- Modern education icons
- Cultural education symbols

**Payment Symbols**:
- Currency symbols (₦, $, abstract)
- Card/digital payment icons
- Transaction flow indicators

### Section E: Emotional Response
**Adjective Selection**:
- Choose 5 words that describe each logo
- Rate emotional intensity (1-10)
- Cultural appropriateness assessment
- Willingness to use platform based on logo

### Section F: Context Testing
**Usage Scenarios**:
- Mobile app icon
- Website header
- Payment confirmation
- School communication
- Marketing materials

**Size Testing**:
- Large format (billboard, website header)
- Medium format (mobile app, business cards)
- Small format (favicon, app icons)

## Analysis Framework

### Quantitative Analysis
- Preference ranking and scoring
- Demographic correlation analysis
- Statistical significance testing
- Cross-tabulation analysis

### Qualitative Analysis
- Thematic analysis of open responses
- Cultural insight extraction
- Emotional response mapping
- Usability concern identification

### Key Metrics
- **Trust Score**: Composite trust perception rating
- **Security Perception**: Security element effectiveness
- **Cultural Resonance**: Nigerian appropriateness rating
- **Professional Appeal**: Business context suitability
- **Approachability**: Parent-friendliness assessment

## Success Criteria

### Primary Success Metrics
- 80%+ participants rate preferred logo as trustworthy
- 70%+ identify security elements correctly
- 75%+ find design culturally appropriate
- Clear preference direction (no split decisions)

### Secondary Success Metrics
- Consistent preferences across demographics
- Strong emotional response to chosen direction
- Clear differentiation from competitors
- Actionable insights for design refinement
