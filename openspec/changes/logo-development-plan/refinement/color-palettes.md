## Color Application Guidelines

### Hierarchy and Priority
**Primary Colors** (80% usage):
- Professional Blue for security elements
- Cultural Gold for achievement elements
- Dark Blue for typography and text

**Secondary Colors** (15% usage):
- Growth Green for educational elements
- Light Blue for backgrounds and interfaces
- Soft Gold for warm accents

**Accent Colors** (5% usage):
- Bright colors for calls-to-action
- Highlight colors for emphasis
- Seasonal or campaign colors

### Contrast Requirements
**Minimum Contrast Ratios**:
- **Normal Text**: 4.5:1 against background
- **Large Text**: 3:1 against background
- **UI Elements**: 3:1 against background
- **Graphics**: 3:1 for adjacent colors

**Recommended Combinations**:
- **Dark Blue on White**: 12.4:1 (Excellent)
- **Cultural Gold on Professional Blue**: 4.5:1 (Good)
- **Growth Green on White**: 4.5:1 (Good)
- **Professional Blue on Light Gray**: 7.2:1 (Excellent)

### Color Psychology by Application

#### Security Applications
**Recommended Colors**:
- Professional Blue (#0066CC) - Primary security
- Dark Blue (#003366) - Security depth
- Navy Blue (#004085) - Serious security

**Avoid**:
- Bright reds (warning, danger)
- Light yellows (caution)
- Orange tones (alert)

#### Educational Applications
**Recommended Colors**:
- Growth Green (#28A745) - Educational growth
- Cultural Gold (#E6A500) - Achievement
- Professional Blue (#0066CC) - Educational trust

**Avoid**:
- Dark grays (depressing)
- Overly bright colors (distracting)
- Muted tones ( uninspiring)

#### Cultural Applications
**Recommended Colors**:
- Nigerian Green (#008751) - National pride
- Cultural Gold (#E6A500) - Cultural celebration
- Nigerian Blue (#0033A0) - National identity

**Avoid**:
- Colors without cultural significance
- Generic international colors
- Culturally inappropriate combinations
